The US being restricted despite PokePay holding a US MSB registration is the most counterintuitive item, but it's actually common: holding an MSB registration lets a company serve customers *from supported regions* who transact in USD, but offering the card to US residents triggers a much heavier set of SEC / FinCEN / state-level money transmitter requirements that most fintechs simply don't take on. PokePay has chosen to skip the US retail market entirely rather than navigate that.

Mainland China being restricted while Hong Kong is the home market reflects the standard HK / mainland regulatory firewall — Hong Kong-licensed financial products generally can't be marketed to mainland residents without separate PBOC / CSRC approvals that few fintechs pursue.

The takeaway for users: if you're in a restricted region, **using a VPN to bypass the IP block violates PokePay's terms of service**, and KYC will likely catch the mismatch between your documents and your declared region.
